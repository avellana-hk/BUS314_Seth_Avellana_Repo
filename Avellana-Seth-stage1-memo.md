# Executive Memo

**To:** Chief Financial Officer  
**From:** Financial Analyst  
**Date:** April 3, 2026  
**Subject:** Value of Comprehensive Accounting Ratio Analysis

I recommend conducting a comprehensive accounting ratio analysis for **Costco Wholesale Corporation**, a publicly traded company in the **retail/warehouse club industry**. Costco operates membership-based warehouse stores that sell a broad mix of merchandise at high volume and relatively low margins. Because the company’s business model depends on efficient inventory movement, disciplined cost control, and steady cash generation, ratio analysis is an effective way to evaluate its recent financial health.

Ratio analysis is valuable because it translates raw financial statement data into decision-useful insights. Income statements, balance sheets, and cash flow statements show absolute results, but they do not fully explain how well the company is performing relative to its asset base, debt levels, operating structure, or short-term obligations. A ratio-based review helps reveal strengths and weaknesses in profitability, operating efficiency, leverage, and liquidity, while also making it easier to identify trends over time and compare performance against peers.

The review will focus on six ratio categories. **Performance ratios** assess how effectively the company turns resources into results for shareholders. **Profitability ratios** measure earnings power and margin quality. **Efficiency ratios** evaluate how productively assets, inventory, and receivables are managed. **Leverage ratios** show the degree to which the business relies on debt financing and its related financial risk. **Liquidity ratios** measure the company’s ability to meet short-term obligations. **Du Pont analysis** breaks return on equity into profit margin, asset turnover, and financial leverage to explain what is driving shareholder returns.

Financial data will be pulled from reliable public sources, including Costco’s **SEC EDGAR 10-K filings**, the company’s **Investor Relations** website, and market data platforms such as **Yahoo Finance** for cross-checking and supplemental reference points. Using multiple sources will help confirm consistency and improve accuracy.

In the next stages, I will build a working Excel model using real reported data to calculate all required ratios. I will then prepare a technical specification documenting the model structure, formulas, and lessons learned. Finally, I will produce an executive analysis memo that interprets the findings, provides recommendations, and includes a structured AI prompt to support future financial analysis workflows.
